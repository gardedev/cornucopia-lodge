What is Freemasonry?

The world's oldest and largest fraternal organization

The Ancient Craft

Freemasonry is the world's oldest and largest fraternal organization, with roots dating back to the medieval stonemasons' guilds. Today, it is a worldwide fraternity of men who are dedicated to the principles of brotherly love, relief, and truth.

Masonry is not a religion, though it requires its members to believe in a Supreme Being. It is not a charity, though it engages in charitable work. It is not a social club, though it fosters lifelong friendships. Freemasonry is a system of morality, veiled in allegory and illustrated by symbols.

Brotherly Love

We regard the entire human species as one family, treating each other with respect and kindness.

Relief

We are committed to helping those in need, both within our fraternity and in the broader community.

Truth

We seek truth in all things, striving to live with honesty and integrity in our daily lives.

Becoming a Mason

To become a Freemason, one must be a man of good character, believe in a Supreme Being, and be recommended by two existing members. The process involves an application, interview, and ballot vote by the lodge membership.

If you are interested in learning more about Freemasonry and Cornucopia Lodge #563, we encourage you to contact us or attend one of our public events.
